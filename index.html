Nasi Lemak

Introduction

Nasi Lemak - This iconic Malaysian dish is all about that fragrant coconut milk rice, topped with crispy anchovies, spicy sambal, hard-boiled eggs, roasted peanuts, and fresh cucumber.

Nasi lemak typically includes fragrant coconut rice, served with a variety of accompaniments. Here’s a basic list of what goes into the recipe.

Recipes

Coconut Rice

  • 2 cups of jasmine rice
  • 1½ cups coconut milk
  • 1½ cups water
  • 1 pandan leaf, tied into a knot
  • 1 tsp salt
  1. Sambal: Spice paste made from dried red chilies, shallots, garlic, and belacan (Malaysian prawn paste). Sambal is usually stir-fried with oil, along with onions and tamarind juice added for tanginess. Salt and sugar are used to balance the flavors.
  2. Dried anchovies (ikan bilis): Small dried anchovies fried until crispy, adding a salty crunch to the dish. In my recipe, I made sambal anchovies, but if you can’t find anchovies, feel free to swap them for Sambal Udang (spicy shrimp).
  3. Peanuts: Roasted peanuts, providing a nutty flavor and crunchy texture.
